Hi Ryan,

Thank you for sharing the Executive Bonus Agreement.

I’m aligned with the intent, and I understand that Series A outcomes depend on investors and market conditions, so I’m not expecting guarantees beyond what’s realistic for a startup.

Before signing, I’d like to clarify several points to ensure expectations are objective and aligned upfront. All documents were reviewed with my personal legal counsel (22+ years of experience), and the same items were flagged as important to address directly in the agreement.

Context (why clarity matters)

Over the past 8 months, I’ve made a substantial commitment to Wigiwork:

  • Compensation trade-off: I joined at $6,000/month, after previously earning $12,000–$13,000/month (including bonuses), and incurred a $9,000 loss from leaving Apple early under a yearly contract.
  • Bonus trade-off: I gave up a $15,000 year-end bonus — the only role in my ~15-year career without one.
  • Sustained workload: Since day one, I’ve worked late nights and full weekends, with no vacations, days off, paid sick leave, or insurance, to keep backend delivery unblocked. I’m currently the sole backend engineer supporting 3 AI engineers and 5–6 frontend engineers.
  • Opportunity cost: The compensation delta over 8 months is approximately $96,000, plus paused side income of roughly $30,000+.
  • Strategic impact: I strongly advocated for Ruby on Rails over Golang, enabling a production-ready API in months rather than mid-next year.
  • Location: I remain based in Ukraine during the ongoing war. Since December 26, following a major rocket attack, there has been no heating, intermittent hot water, and unstable electricity.

Given the sustained base-compensation reduction, the bonus functions as a component of total compensation rather than a discretionary perk, which is why clarity here is particularly important.

Points requiring clarification

1) Series A target and valuation basis

The bonus tiers are anchored to a $50M Series A, but the agreement does not clarify how the feasibility of that target is determined. Without an objective basis, it is possible for the market-supported outcome to be $20–30M, resulting in 50% or zero bonus.

I want to explicitly separate individual performance from fundraising outcomes, which are driven by market conditions rather than execution alone.

2) “Satisfactory performance, as solely determined by the Company”

In a bonus agreement tied to a material amount, this formulation is problematic. As written, performance is not measured against KPIs or OKRs and depends entirely on unilateral determination.

3) Bad Leaver / “harms the Company’s interests”

If burnout or performance degradation results primarily from structural staffing decisions, it would be concerning if such outcomes could later be treated as unsatisfactory performance or harm to the Company’s interests.

Closing

What I’m asking for is not guarantees, but clear, objective, and balanced terms consistent with executive-level incentives.

If aligning on all bonus-related points takes more time, I see a pragmatic way to restore balance and keep execution healthy:

  • Base compensation adjustment: A +25% increase to my current base salary to offset prolonged compensation reduction and stabilize my family budget.
  • Remote weekend work arrangement: I’m prepared to continue weekend support when needed, but remotely, as a 2.5-hour daily commute is not sustainable.

I genuinely like the team and the product, and I’m fully committed to building Wigiwork. I’d appreciate aligning on this before execution continues much further.

Thank you for taking the time to review this.
